# Tiny Habits

### 1. what was the most interesting story or idea for you?

When you fail at a habit, you shouldn't blame your willpower. Instead, blame the design of the habit and adjust the steps to make it easier.

### 2. How can you use B = MAP to make making new habits easier? What are M, A and P.

Behavior (B) happens when Motivation (M), Ability (A), and a Prompt (P) come together at the same time.
* M (Motivation): Don't rely on it because it changes daily.
* A (Ability): Shrink the habit to a 30-second version so it takes zero effort.
* P (Prompt): Do the new tiny habit immediately after an existing daily routine.

### 3. Why celebrate ("Shine") after a habit?

When you celebrate right away like saying "I'm awesome!", your brain releases dopamine. This good feeling wires your brain to want to repeat the habit.

### 4. what was the most interesting story or idea for you?

The idea of the "Plateau of Latent Potential." Think of heating an ice cube: you heat it from 26°F to 31°F, and nothing seems to happen. Then, at 32°F, it suddenly starts to melt. Tiny 1% changes feel useless at first because your progress is being stored up, not wasted. The big breakthrough only happens after you push through this quiet phase.

### 5. What is the book's perspective about Identity?

True change is about who you believe you are, not just what you want to achieve. Instead of focusing on the outcome, focus on the identity. Every good action is a vote for the type of person you want to become.

### 6. Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit easier to do?

If you want to exercise in the morning, put your shoes right next to your bed the night before. If you want to eat fruit, put it right on the kitchen table. By making the good choice the easiest thing to see and grab, you don't even have to think about it.

### 7. Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit harder to do?

Use a Habit Contract and an Accountability Partner. Make a binding promise with a friend or partner that if you skip your habit, you face an immediate, painful consequence—like giving them $50 or doing their chores. The fear of public embarrassment and instant loss makes the bad habit too costly to repeat.

### 8. What are the steps that you can take to make it make the cue obvious or the habit more attractive or easy and or response satisfying?

* Keep that bottle sitting right next to your computer screen all day.
* Take just one sip every time you finish reading.
* Give yourself a quick mental high-five after every refill.

### 9. What are the steps that you can take to make it make the cue invisible or the process unattractive or hard or the response unsatisfying?

The biggest takeaway is that your environment and system matter way more than your willpower. If you are struggling to build a good habit or break a bad one, stop beating yourself up. Instead, change your surroundings, shrink the step down to 30 seconds, and fix the system so success becomes the easiest option.
