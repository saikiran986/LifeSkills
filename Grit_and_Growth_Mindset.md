# Grit and Growth Mindset:

### 1. Grit

Grit is simply having a long-term goal and refusing to give up on it. It’s about practicing stamina and treating life like a marathon, not a quick sprint. The video shows that working hard and staying dedicated matters way more for success than raw talent or a high IQ.


### 2. Introduction to Growth Mindset

A growth mindset is believing that you can get smarter and better at things if you put in the effort and practice. Instead of thinking "I'm just naturally bad at this," a growth mindset makes you look at mistakes and hard tasks as normal ways to learn, not as a sign that you failed.


### 3. What is the Internal Locus of Control? What is the key point in the video?

* It’s the belief that you are the driver of your own life. You believe that your choices and hard work dictate your results, rather than blaming outside things like luck, the weather, or other people.
* To stay motivated, you have to feel like you are in control. When you realize that your actions actually change your reality, you naturally want to keep trying.


### 4. What are the key points mentioned by speaker to build growth mindset?


* Believe you can figure things out.
* Question your negative assumptions about what you can or can't do.
* Create your own "life curriculum" (keep reading, learning, and taking classes on your own).
* "Honor the struggle" — accept that the hard, frustrating parts of learning are exactly what make you stronger.


### 5. What are your ideas to take action and build Growth Mindset?


* The next time you get stuck on a difficult problem and think, "I can't do this", quickly add the word yet to the end of the sentence. "I don't know how to do this... yet."
* If someone corrects your work, don't look at it as an attack on your intelligence. See it as a free cheat code to get better faster.
* When you meet a major roadblock, force yourself to try and solve it by researching and experimenting for 20 minutes before asking for help. Treat that frustration like a workout for your brain.
*  At the end of a hard day, look back and be proud of how hard you tried and what you learned, even if the final result wasn't totally perfect.
